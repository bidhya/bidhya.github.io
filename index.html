    Bidhyananda Yadav

    Geocomputational Scientist  ·  Spatial Data Engineer  ·  ML/AI Researcher

    Seven years designing TB-scale Earth observation pipelines, deep learning systems, and HPC workflows for NASA and NSF programmes at The Ohio State University.

    Engineering Notes Technical Workflows

    What I Build

    • Geospatial & Remote Sensing Pipelines

      Processing optical, SAR, LiDAR, and hyperspectral satellite data at TB scale. Automated ETL pipelines for continental coverage built on Xarray, Rioxarray, GDAL, and GeoPandas.

    • Machine Learning & AI Systems

      Deep learning architectures in PyTorch for water classification and super-resolution imaging. Fine-tuning geospatial foundation models (Prithvi, Clay) with LoRA/PEFT for Arctic and hydrological targets.

    • HPC & Cloud Infrastructure

      Distributed SLURM cluster workflows and AWS-native pipelines (Lambda, S3, containers). Multi-GPU DDP training. Designed for reproducibility and seamless HPC-to-cloud migration at scale.

    Featured Research

    • NASA SWOT Satellite Mission

      Co-led remote sensing algorithms for global river discharge and lake dynamics. Built and maintain the worldwide SWOT analytics dashboard (swotviz.cuahsi.io) serving international hydrology teams.

      NASA Grant · $925,353 · 2022–2024

    • Continental Snow Water Equivalent

      Architected a 20-year daily SWE data product for North America at 0.01° resolution (~20 TB). Fused NASA MODIS/VIIRS observations with land surface model outputs on HPC clusters.

      NSF Collaborator · 2019–2024

    • Deep Learning Water Classification

      Designed PyTorch super-resolution DNN architectures to classify water bodies from Sentinel-2 and commercial high-resolution imagery. Published in Journal of Hydrology and Remote Sensing of Environment.

    • ICESat-2 Elevation Toolkit

      Open-source Python toolset for continental-scale DEM bias correction (ArcticDEM, REMA, WorldDEM) using ICESat-2 laser altimetry. Automated pipelines reduced processing from weeks to days.
